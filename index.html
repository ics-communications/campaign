The Challenge

We live in a time of deep division.

Public life is marked by suspicion and contempt. Our institutions feel strained. Even ordinary relationships fray under cultural and political pressure.

The church has not been immune. A community called to embody peace has too often mirrored the culture of blame and exclusion. The welcome Jesus extends toward the disadvantaged, the vulnerable, and the outsider is easily obscured when we build walls and close doors.

A Crossroads

Christians like you are asking what faithfulness requires now.

In public life, in our institutions, and in the formation of leaders, the questions are urgent. If you long for a faith that is spacious enough to meet this moment, your partnership can form the leaders who carry it forward.

The Response

A spacious faith is not thin or vague.

It is spiritually alive, intellectually serious, and rooted in grace and courage. It makes room for honest questions, deep learning, and wounded people who need a place to belong.

For more than fifty years, with partners like you, the Institute for Christian Studies has cultivated this kind of faith through interdisciplinary graduate programs that join rigorous inquiry with faith seeking understanding, forming scholars, educators, pastors, and public leaders who carry it forward.
